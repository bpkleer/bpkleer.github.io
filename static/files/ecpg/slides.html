Why deal with minority groups?

Especially for minority groups, ‘participation in decision-making has both instrumental and intrinsic value in challenging oppression and domination (…), (since) it is instrumentally valuable because it means that all interests will be voiced’ (Fletcher, 1998, pp. 203–204).

Research question and aim

Comparing general population and LGBTQ+ population in voting behavior? Find differences and similarities.

Incorporating knowledge of general population into LGBTQ+ models and check if this prior knowledge imrpoves model estimation on LGBTQ+

  • Intersectional effects: problem especially among LGBTQ+ study cause of snowball sampling: low cases of lower educated, immigrants

Tricks and Ruses

How we made it work .

What we used

  • DV: voting behavior (voting for SPD, voting for Greens)1
  • Gender, age, education, immigration, satisfaction with own economic situation, political interest, political participation and engagement, East/West Germany, support for gender equality and support for income distribution

logistic models of vote choice for SPD, and the Greens

using Bayesian statistics (incorporating priors)

posterior predictive checks and LOO-IC comparisons.

  1. We checked also for FDP and the Left.

Ruses

  • gender: restricted to binary (male/female)
  • education: restricted to higher education vs. all other
  • political participation and engagement: formal engagement in parties or unions
  • immigration: only first generation immigration ( conflicts with eligibility)

simplified model of voting behavior

  • gender: no measurement in GLES, in LGBTQ+ also low cases for non-binary (several categories)
  • education: low cases for lower education in LGBTQ+, not possible especially for building interactions/intersections
  • political participation and engagement: only measurements that are framed in the same way across data sets
  • immigration: only first generation, therefore, low n

Do straight knowledge help explaining LGBTQ+s’ behavior?

Do LGBs vote differently?

  • we see bold left-wing attitudes, but only towards the left an greens, SPD less likely

  • however, self-selective/snowball samples

  • framing of study leans towards left-wing LGBTQ+; conductors are affiliated with SPD

  • difficulties probably predicting SPD (due to modelling intersections)

Discussion

Although models exhibit some similarity, there are some clear differences:

  • effects of political engagement (SPD voters)

  • effects of age, support gender equality, tax the rich (Greens voters)

General population knowledge does not help to improve models (but also not worsened models)

  • research especially on this population needed

difficulties in approaching similar variables

  • need more standardize items in LGBTQ* Voting (comparability with general population)
